The Unraveling

[ Written in 2019 ]

When I was 23, I was in a particularly bad relationship. This poem is about acknowledging and processing the trauma, and forgiving myself for my role in it.

I am content and happy now, and every day feels like a blessing. However, the effects linger and I come back to this poem sometimes. Yoga has really helped me, and I am starting tantric yoga to help release sexual trauma.



Slowly undo the sadness, not to overwhelm the senses.

Comb your fingers over every wound,

Remembering where each came from —

Every lie, betrayal, and cutting word whittled into you.

Understand they hurt because they hurt,

from a place of desperation

and reasons beyond your comprehension.

Let love and grace fill the empty spaces,

Then feel that heaving in your chest steady

to a shaky breath —

Sustained grief is particularly disturbing in a culture that offers a quick fix for pain. Sometimes it amazes me to know intuitively that the grieving are all around us yet we do not see any overt signs of their anguished spirits. We are taught to feel shame about grief that lingers. Like a stain on our clothes, it marks us as flawed, imperfect. To cling to grief, to desire its expression, is to be out of sync with modern life, where the hip do not get bogged down in mourning.

Trust your gut and think for yourself.
