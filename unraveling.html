Written sometime in 2019.

This poem is about healing old wounds, through peeling back the layers and "going through the jungle," so to speak. There was much more to this poem — anger, spitefulness, and despair directed at people I cared about — but those feelings come and go. The first stanza always resonates. This one's about learning to self-regulate while unpacking trauma.

I experienced a lot of emotional flashbacks in 2019-2020 due to the aftermath of an abusive relationship, and reading this poem is like a recalibration exercise which helps me separate myself from someone else's actions. To anyone reading this: you did not "let" bad things happen to you. My current partner told me that a good person, despite being given the choice to do something bad, will choose the good. So simple and obvious.

Trust your gut and stick to your morals. Seek trauma-informed therapy. Call a bitch out when you need to.



The Unraveling


Slowly undo the sadness, not to overwhelm the senses.

Comb your fingers over every wound,

remembering where each came from —

Every lie, betrayal, and cutting word whittled into you.

Understand they hurt because they hurt,

from a place of desperation

and reasons beyond your comprehension.

Let love and grace fill the empty spaces,

Then feel that heaving in your chest steady

to a shaky breath —
