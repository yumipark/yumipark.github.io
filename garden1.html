The Garden:

This winter will be spent planning a forest garden for the backyard. Originally, I wanted a traditional garden with raised garden beds and neat rows of vegetables. After reading Braiding Sweetgrass and doing a bit of research online, planting a forest garden seems like the best option. A variety of plants (tall trees, low trees, shrubs, herbs, ground cover, roots, and vines) are planted to mimic a natural forest. They're more sustainable and require less upkeep. 

I'll make a list of plants already living with us and the ones that I want transplanted here, and I'll head to a native plant nursery the next town over come spring. For ground cover, I want to plant wild strawberries — and I also want to plant goldenrods and asters together. Robin Kimmerer was a big influence and I will follow her guidelines for an honorable harvest:


Know the ways of the ones who take care of you, so that you may take care of them.

Introduce yourself. Be accountable as the one who comes asking for life. Ask permission before taking. Abide by the answer.

Never take the first. Never take the last. Take only what you need.

Take only that which is given.

Never take more than half. Leave some for others. Harvest in a way that minimizes harm.

Use it respectfully. Never waste what you have taken. Share.

Give thanks for what you have been given.

Give a gift, in reciprocity for what you have taken.

Sustain the ones who sustain you and the earth will last forever.


— Robin Wall Kimmerer, Braiding Sweetgrass
